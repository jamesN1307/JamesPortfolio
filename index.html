My name is James Nguyen

3 years ago after I graduated from the University of Washington with the Bachelor of Science in Physiology I wanted to pursue health care as a profession. Unfortunately a year after I graduated the world as we known enter a pandemic and every plan and project that I have for Pharmacy School got put on hold. However, while the majority of people in America lost their job I was very fortunate to be able to keep my job since I was working in the clinical frontline providing healthcare services. However, those 12 months that I work during the pandemic was what most health care providers described as trauma, nightmare and exhaustion. We see everything, from people drinking fish tank cleaning product to people stealing tylenol and aspirin, ignorant individual come to harass pharmacist because they believe that the covid vaccines to contain a magnetic tracking chip that connects to 5G that the government use to track them. Yes, we see it all. The health care information the general public on the internet is often inaccurate, and the misinformation may lead to patient harm and significant expense of healthcare dollars. Technological implementation has been proven effective for error prevention and medication stewardship. Healthcare informatics however remains untouched. My passion for helping people remain unchange, however I want to help educate and fix all the misinformation that available on the internet. My goal is to excel in the skills of programming, troubleshooting, and software development, and be able to deliver meaningful projects across two professions. I am self-driven and detail-oriented. I am eager to learn new skills and computer languages, and open for any constructive criticism. For the future, the goal is to be a healthcare engineer.
